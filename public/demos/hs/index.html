FAQs

Frequently asked questions

I'm stuck at the roadside, what should I do?
First, get yourself somewhere safe, put your hazard lights on and stand away from the traffic. Then give us a call. We answer day and night, we'll stay on the phone with you if you'd like, and we're usually with you in 30 to 60 minutes.
What do I actually need to tell you?
Honestly, not much. Just roughly where you are (a postcode, a what3words pin, or even a nearby shop or landmark is fine) and what's happened, like a flat, a blowout or a slow puncture. Not sure of the details? Don't worry, sorting that out is our job.
I don't know my tyre size, is that a problem?
Not at all. If you can read the numbers on the side of your tyre (something like 205/55 R16) it helps us bring the right one, but if you can't find them we'll check for you when we arrive. Just call and we'll talk you through it.
How much will it cost?
We'll always give you a clear, honest price before we start any work, so there are no nasty surprises. We're known locally for being one of the most competitive around, and you can pay by card or cash once the job's done.
Are you really open 24/7?
Yes, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year, including evenings, weekends and bank holidays. Flat tyres don't keep office hours, and neither do we.
Can you fit tyres I've already bought?
Yes, no problem at all. Bought them online, or got a set sitting in the boot? We'll fit them for you, including a new valve and wheel balancing.
Are your puncture repairs proper repairs?
Yes. Every puncture we repair is done to the official British Standard (BS AU 159), so it's a safe, permanent fix that lasts the legal life of the tyre, not a quick bodge.
Do I need to be there?
It's best if you can be there to hand over your keys, but if you're at home or work we can often be flexible. Just let us know your situation when you call and we'll work around you.
What happens to my old tyre?
Leave it with us. We'll get rid of it safely and responsibly for a small fee, so you don't have to think about a trip to the tip.
