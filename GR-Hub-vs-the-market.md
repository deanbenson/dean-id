# What a full estate-agency system does — and what the G.R. Hub should do

*Researched across the main UK agency systems (Reapit, Alto, Jupix, Street, Dezrez, agentOS…), modern agency websites + client portals, and the 2024-25 AI/proptech wave. Then mapped onto G.R.*

## Short answer to "it's a full-blown agency system, isn't it?"
Yes — in effect the Hub becomes **G.R.'s agency operating system.** But the smart version, and the one that keeps us fast and low-risk: **we do not rebuild the CRM.** Street already does the heavy core well (contacts, pipeline, lettings, client money, compliance) and it's the system of record. The Hub is the **layer on top**: one G.R.-owned place that (1) unifies every system into a single pane of glass, (2) adds the front-of-house edge the big systems haven't operationalised, and (3) gives the owner cross-everything visibility Street is weaker at. That's a stronger position than a from-scratch system — it leapfrogs straight to the parts that win instructions.

## Every module a comprehensive agency system has
**Sales / front-office:** contacts & applicant CRM · sales pipeline · sales progression & chain management · property/listing record · applicant matching · diary/viewings · 24/7 online booking.
**Lettings & property management:** tenancy lifecycle · referencing (credit / Right-to-Rent) · maintenance & works orders · inventory (check-in/out) · rent collection & arrears chasing · deposit protection · key management.
**Money & compliance:** client accounts / client money · landlord statements & payouts · accounting export (Xero/QuickBooks) · certificate tracking (Gas/EICR/EPC) · AML/KYC on buyers & vendors.
**Marketing & distribution:** portal upload (Rightmove/Zoopla/OTM) · single enquiry inbox · brochures/window cards · email/SMS/WhatsApp campaigns · the website.
**Client portals (one branded, role-aware app):** vendor · landlord · buyer · tenant.
**Cross-cutting:** reporting & analytics · tasks/workflow · mobile agent app · multi-branch admin · integrations / open API.

## The public website + client portals (what consumers now expect)
**Public site:** instant online valuation → book a human appraisal (the lead funnel) · saved properties & searches · property alerts ("be first") · online viewing booking · mortgage & stamp-duty calculators · area guides + sold prices · video/360 tours · WhatsApp/chat · reviews & accreditation badges.
**Client portal (single login, tailored by role):** vendor sees viewing feedback, offers, a progression timeline; landlord sees a portfolio dashboard, statements, maintenance approvals, a certificate vault; buyer books viewings, submits offers, uploads AML/proof-of-funds; tenant does onboarding (referencing, Right-to-Rent, e-sign), reports maintenance (with AI triage), pays rent. Best practice (Street's model) is **one branded app that detects the role**, not four portals.

## The AI layer — where an independent can out-execute the chains
Only ~24% of small independents use AI vs ~82% of big chains. That gap is the opening.
**Already table-stakes** (absence is now a negative): AI listing descriptions · website instant-valuation capture · a basic chatbot · automated vendor updates & viewing feedback · email/SMS nurture sequences · AVM-backed appraisal prep.
**Still genuine differentiators** (mostly only big chains have them): **24/7 AI voice receptionist + sub-minute call-back on new enquiries** · AI-native sales progression that flags stalls · multi-channel nurture (incl. WhatsApp) tied to lead scoring · **owner BI you can query in plain language** · reputation automation tuned for Google *and* AI-search visibility · custom/agentic workflows.
**Compliance to bake in:** since Feb 2025 (TPO), any AI valuation must be presented as **"AI-assisted, human-verified"** with method disclosed. Disclosure isn't just compliance — agents who disclose AI see ~80% consumer approval vs ~54% who don't. So it's a trust feature.

## Street: what we lean on vs where we add value
**Lean on Street (don't rebuild):** the CRM core, client accounting, lettings compliance, branded client apps, AML integrations, portal upload, AI onboarding/descriptions, and — crucially for us — its **open API + webhooks** (which is exactly why a hub can sit on top; we've already proven live read *and* write).
**Where the Hub adds value Street is weaker at:** cross-branch **reporting/BI**, **unifying** data Street parks in partner tools (maintenance, inventory, referencing) into one view, **custom workflows** Street can't express, and the **front-of-house edge** (our website + AI front desk + reviews/nurture), all in one **G.R.-owned** place.

## Suggested priority order (from the table-stakes vs differentiator split)
1. **Finish the website's lead edge** — instant valuation → booking, saved homes + alerts. Table-stakes lead-gen we're missing.
2. **The AI front desk** — the rare differentiator; phone + web, 24/7, into Street, measured in the Hub.
3. **Owner BI in the Hub** — Street's weak spot, our strength; cross-branch at a glance.
4. **Reviews + nurture automation** — easy table-stakes wins.
5. **Decide the client-portal stance** — lean on Street's apps vs build our own.
6. **Per-user logins** — the foundational access build (already designed).

## Our unfair advantages
Independent, so we move fast. We **own** the tech (not renting a chain's stack). And we've already done the hard part: live Street read+write, live reviews, and the Hub shell. The chains have scale; we have speed and ownership. The flagship to chase is the **24/7 AI front desk** — rare even among the big CRMs, and the clearest way for a Teesside independent to be the most responsive agent in the patch.
