## The angle (why local press will care)
An **award-winning, independent** Teesside agency is doing something the big corporate chains charge a fortune for and most independents never attempt: building its **own** technology — a website that talks to its CRM in real time, live reviews across every branch, and an AI assistant that answers questions day and night. The story isn't "agent gets a new website." It's **"local independent out-innovates the nationals, on its own terms, owning its own tech."** That's a David-and-Goliath local-business story, which is exactly what regional press and business pages like.

Secondary hooks: jobs/growth signal, Teesside pride, and a "what's next" teaser (v3) so there's a second story later.

## Launch as **v2**, tease **v3**
- **v2 (launch now):** the new site — faster, live listings, live reviews, Ask Georgina (AI), removals, all branches. This is real and shippable.
- **v3 (teased):** "My G.R. Estates" — saved homes, instant *be-first* alerts when a matching home lands, saved searches, and the AI concierge. Framed as *coming soon* so we earn a second PR moment when it ships.

Why two beats: one launch = one headline. Launch + roadmap = two headlines and a sense of momentum.
